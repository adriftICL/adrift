Where does all the plastic go?

Most plastic is lighter than water so it floats on the surface and is swept around by ocean currents. A lot of plastic gets caught in spiralling ocean currents called ocean gyres. It accumulates in these gyres, creating garbage vortices (or garbage ‘patches’ in the ocean). The circular motion of the gyre draws the debris into the centre where it becomes trapped and builds up.

Is it the only garbage patch in the ocean?

No, each ocean has its own accumulation of marine debris. There are 5 ocean gyres, and so there are 5 ocean patches.
