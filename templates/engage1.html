Land sources of plastic

80% of the plastic that ends up in the ocean comes from the land. This plastic usually makes the journey from land to sea via drains, particularly storm water drains. Rubbish that is thrown onto the street washes into the drains when it rains. The rain water travels through these drains and ends up in the ocean, taking the rubbish with it.

But some of that plastic from land comes directly from the beach. Sadly, people leave rubbish at the beach after a day in the sun. The waves and the tide can take it up from there and sweep it into the ocean.

Factory waste

Plastic waste also comes from factories, often in the places where the plastic is made. Factories are often located near coastlines so that ships can come and go easily. This means the waste that flows from factories makes a very short journey to the ocean.

Boats and ships at sea

Approximately 20% of the marine debris floating around in the ocean comes from boats and ships at sea. Fishing vessels often loose fishing lines and nets overboard, either from bad weather or through intentional dumping. Extreme storms at sea can cause containers on large shipping vessels to fall overboard, spilling their contents straight into the ocean. In 1992 a container filled with thousands of plastic rubber ducks was lost at sea in a very heavy storm. The plastic toys ares still being found along the coastlines today.
